EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Global markets exhibit a mixed tone today as geopolitical escalation in the Middle East competes with softening US inflation signals. The critical focal point centers on the Strait of Hormuz conflict, which threatens global energy flows and prompts warnings of an impending economic shock. While energy markets react with caution, equity and currency markets remain highly sensitive to incoming central bank commentary and macroeconomic data.


MACRO & CENTRAL BANKS

Federal Reserve Chairman Kevin Warsh delivers testimony to the Senate banking committee, addressing interest rates and the broader economy. Soft US PPI data for June provides temporary relief from persistent inflation worries, though regional data elsewhere remains mixed. Italy confirms its June inflation rate at 3.0%, showing only a marginal easing in core prices. Meanwhile, the United Kingdom economy registers a marginal GDP growth of 0.1% in May, supported by a 0.3% bounce in the services sector.


EQUITIES

Asian equities experience notable selling pressure, with the Nikkei and Kospi sliding sharply alongside slumping semiconductor stocks. Market sentiment is further divided over the sustainability of the artificial intelligence boom, with some critics warning of an impending bubble collapse while others note its unavoidable dominance in corporate credit. European and US futures point to a cautious, mixed open as traders weigh geopolitical risk against corporate earnings. TotalEnergies, however, expects stronger second-quarter profits driven by surging refining margins and tight fuel markets.


COMMODITIES & ENERGY

Crude markets digest ongoing tensions as the United States launches fresh military strikes against Iranian targets along the southern coast, though Brent prices show minimal immediate reaction. Concurrently, Ukraine systematically disrupts Russian refining capacity to a 21-year low, forcing Moscow to ban fuel exports. European natural gas prices hold at 15-week highs, and Pakistan pays its highest spot liquefied natural gas price in four years due to Middle East supply disruptions. Additionally, geopolitical strikes on Black Sea vessels drive global wheat prices higher, while gold remains in the red as inflation fears persist.


CRYPTO

Ethereum outpaces Bitcoin as institutional capital flows back into digital assets, led primarily by BlackRock's ETF. On-chain analysis from Glassnode highlights the $69,000 level as a crucial battleground, with long-term buyers targeting a potential 2026 bear-market bottom. Regulatory actions ramp up as Tether freezes $131 million linked to four sanctioned Iranian central bank crypto wallets. In decentralized finance, ARK Invest disputes claims that traditional finance will avoid open DeFi protocols in favor of permissioned blockchains.


FOREX & RATES

The US Dollar remains steady as soft domestic inflation prints counter the geopolitical risk premium stemming from the Middle East. Euro yields trend higher as energy-driven inflation fears weigh on European debt, while Bank of America forecasts further Euro weakness against the Australian and New Zealand dollars. The Eurozone trade deficit widens significantly to €7.8 billion in May due to surging energy import costs. Meanwhile, the British Pound holds steady following the in-line domestic GDP growth report.


GEOPOLITICAL RISK

The International Energy Agency warns that the global economy has only weeks to avoid a severe shock if the Strait of Hormuz remains closed to tanker traffic. Iran's military issues warnings that continued United States airstrikes will cause the conflict to spread to new regional areas. In Eastern Europe, military actions continue to target maritime vessels in the Black Sea, threatening grain shipping routes. These dual conflicts elevate systemic risk across international supply chains and commodity trading routes.


MARKET OUTLOOK

Traders should closely monitor physical energy markets over the next 24 to 48 hours for signs of actual supply disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz. Technical levels for Bitcoin at the $69,000 mark represent key near-term support and resistance. Continued central bank rhetoric and the fallout from Federal Reserve testimony will likely dictate the next direction for Treasury yields and the US Dollar. Finally, upcoming corporate earnings and semiconductor price actions will determine if the equity market sell-off stabilizes.
