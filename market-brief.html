EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Global markets exhibit mixed sentiment today as geopolitical tensions in the Middle East drive high volatility, counterbalanced by pockets of bullish momentum in equities and cryptocurrencies. The primary market narrative centers on the conflict in the Strait of Hormuz and its direct transmission into energy prices and risk-off asset demand. While safe-haven flows bolster the US dollar and gold, technical breakout attempts in Bitcoin and a stabilization in semiconductor futures prevent a broader market sell-off.


MACRO & CENTRAL BANKS

The United Kingdom labor market reports a data beat, showing resilient employment but cooling wage growth that offers room for the Bank of England to consider rate cuts. In the Eurozone, the German ZEW survey points to an improving recovery sentiment for July, though regional banks continue to tighten lending standards amid elevated geopolitical risks. Meanwhile, JPMorgan Chase CEO Jamie Dimon warns that markets are significantly underestimating macroeconomic risks, stating he avoids buying equities or Treasurys at current valuations.


EQUITIES

Wall Street futures point higher as semiconductor stocks lead a recovery following a recent 20% slide in the sector. Traditional equity markets face growing competition from digital platforms, prompting the London Stock Exchange to announce plans for a 24-hour trading venue by 2027. Investors maintain a cautious stance ahead of major technology earnings, balancing recent chip-sector volatility against broader macroeconomic uncertainties.


COMMODITIES & ENERGY

Brent crude experiences highly volatile trading, peaking above $90 per barrel after a projectile strikes a Kuwaiti tanker in the Strait of Hormuz, before easing to $88.77 on reports of potential US-Iran ceasefire talks. Goldman Sachs warns that a prolonged closure of the Hormuz strait could push oil prices up to $120 per barrel by the end of the year. Concurrently, natural gas prices hover near multi-month highs, forcing countries like Pakistan to pay record-high spot LNG prices due to disrupted Qatari supplies.


CRYPTO

Bitcoin leads a broader digital asset rally, gaining to $66.3K as a successful range breakout sparks a new one-month high. Regulatory and institutional milestones continue as Tether Gold gains spot commodity recognition in Abu Dhabi, while Grayscale files an S-1 for a Worldcoin ETF. However, regulatory pressures persist in Asia, where retail users in Vietnam face $1,900 fines for trading on offshore exchanges like Binance and OKX.


FOREX & RATES

The US Dollar index gains support from safe-haven demand, causing the British pound to weaken despite a positive UK jobs report. The Canadian dollar experiences modest selling pressure following comments by Carney regarding readiness for trade negotiations with the US amid new tariff threats. Elevated Treasury yields reflect ongoing market anxiety, while South Korea's policymakers flag potential weakness in the won.


GEOPOLITICAL RISK

The Strait of Hormuz remains the primary geopolitical flashpoint as military escalations threaten critical global shipping lanes and energy supply security. US-Iran relations face a critical juncture as Trump nears a pivotal decision on Iran, while concurrent reports surface of secret ceasefire negotiations. In North Asia, trade tensions linger as Canada braces for trade talks following Washington's latest tariff implementation.


MARKET OUTLOOK

Traders should closely monitor the $90 per barrel resistance level on Brent crude and the $66.3K support level on Bitcoin to gauge near-term momentum. Key risks over the next 24 to 48 hours center on the confirmation of any diplomatic progress between the US and Iran, which could rapidly deflate the oil risk premium. Additionally, upcoming technology earnings and further details on the US CLARITY bill's consumer protection additions will dictate risk appetite across equities and digital assets.
