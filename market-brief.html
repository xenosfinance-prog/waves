Eurozone sovereign bond yields climb as the crude oil price spike triggers renewed hawkish expectations for the central bank. Historical trends suggest that equities typically require another week of corporate reporting before earnings fully drive sustained market moves.


COMMODITIES & ENERGY

Brent crude oil trades above 90 dollars per barrel as ongoing US-Iran strikes shut down LNG shipments and disrupt oil tanker traffic through the Strait of Hormuz. European natural gas prices surge 3 percent to multi-month highs in response to a tanker fire in the Persian Gulf, while China's LNG imports jump 8.3 percent to 5.68 million tons. High energy costs severely impact major consumers, driving India's crude import bill up by 60 percent year-on-year in the April-June quarter. Conversely, Europe avoids a feared summer jet fuel shortage, showing the resilience and adaptability of regional energy supply chains.

FOREX & RATES

The Euro and US Dollar pause their recent movements as traders position themselves ahead of major central bank policy meetings later in the week. The British Pound edges up slightly, but remains vulnerable as elevated energy prices keep the US Dollar bid due to Persian Gulf supply risks. The Japanese Yen finds digital payment integration as logistics company AZ-Com Maruwa announces plans to adopt the JPYC stablecoin for driver payments. Rising Eurozone bond yields reflect growing market anxiety that energy-driven inflation will delay expected interest rate cuts.


GEOPOLITICAL RISK

US and Iranian forces continue trading strikes in the Middle East, with Iran asserting that its sovereign rights over the Strait of Hormuz are non-negotiable. Iran's Foreign Ministry suggests diplomatic channels remain open through mediators, stating that a binary choice between negotiations and war is not useful. In Eastern Europe, a Russian attack on a corn vessel near Odesa, Ukraine, results in 10 casualties, underscoring ongoing risks to Black Sea agricultural exports. These combined geopolitical flashpoints keep risk premiums elevated across all major asset classes.


MARKET OUTLOOK

Market participants must monitor the Strait of Hormuz situation over the next 24 to 48 hours for any signs of military escalation or diplomatic progress. Key upcoming drivers include the rollout of megacap corporate earnings in the US and policy clues from major central bank meetings. Technical traders are closely watching Brent crude to see if it sustains levels above the 90 dollar mark, alongside Bitcoin's ability to hold its critical support trend line. Expected Eurozone and German economic releases will provide further clues on the trajectory of regional yields and the Euro.
