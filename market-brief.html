Meanwhile, Ukrainian attacks on Russian refineries disrupt fuel supplies, plunging Central Asian nations into an energy crisis. China also secures its long-term energy interests by signing eight cooperation agreements with Namibia covering critical minerals and infrastructure.


CRYPTO

Bitcoin maintains its July gains of nearly 10 percent, holding above the 64,000 USD level despite warnings from traders of a potential bear market return in August. Ethereum climbs 3 percent driven by an institutional tokenization boom, although weak on-chain and derivatives data leaves the asset vulnerable to a 1,700 USD retest. On the corporate side, treasury company Empery Digital reduces its exposure by selling approximately half of its Bitcoin holdings.


FOREX & RATES

The Japanese Yen surges on anticipated domestic policy adjustments, while the US Dollar heads toward a marginal weekly gain. An IMF working paper notes that dollar-denominated stablecoins can improve foreign exchange access but warns they risk coordinating exits from local currencies during severe exchange-rate stress. Concurrently, intense summer heatwaves across Europe stress local power grids and threaten nuclear plant operations, driving up regional utility demands and influencing euro-zone fiscal expectations.


GEOPOLITICAL RISK

Geopolitical tensions spike as the United States issues fresh sanctions against Iran following renewed military strikes in the Middle East. In response to these supply threats, China instructs its domestic refiners to maintain high fuel output and halts helium exports to protect its semiconductor supply chain. Further complicating regional dynamics, Kazakhstan extends its petroleum export ban for six months as gasoline shortages tighten their grip in neighboring Russia.


MARKET OUTLOOK

Over the next 24 to 48 hours, traders must monitor energy price action as Brent crude tests key technical resistance levels above 76 USD amid the ongoing Strait of Hormuz bottleneck. Market participants should watch for further developments in U.S.-Iran relations and any retaliatory actions that could trigger broader supply disruptions. Additionally, the foreign exchange market remains highly sensitive to upcoming Japanese policy updates and the dollar index reaction to spring inflation commentary.
